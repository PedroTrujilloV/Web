Getting here

click to show/hide ▼▲



Cartagena is not as far away as you may think! The flight distance between Orlando and Cartagena is about the same as between Orlando and Minneapolis.

The budget airline Spirit flies from Orlando to Cartagena, connecting in Fort Lauderdale (FLL). At last check, round trip fares between Orlando and Cartagena were less than $400 on Spirit, so many guests are flying out on Spirit the morning of Friday, January 16th and coming back the afternoon of Tuesday, January 20th.

JetBlue has a direct flight (less than 4 hours) from Orlando (MCO) to Bogota, Colombia (BOG). From Bogota, COPA, LAN, Avianca, and VivaColombia fly (90 minutes) to Cartagena.

You can also fly on Avianca from Orlando to Cartagena with one connection in Bogota. Avianca also flies directly from Miami (MIA) to Cartagena (CTG).

If you have frequent flier miles on United, you may be able to find space on Avianca or COPA since they are part of the Star Alliance. If you have American Airlines miles, check flights with LAN Colombia since they are part of oneworld.

Rafael Núñez International Airport is only a 10 minute drive from the heart of Cartagena's old walled city, and a taxi will cost less than 10 USD. Renting a car is not recommended, and would be much more trouble than it's worth with in this walkable city with limited parking and cheap taxis.

Remember that Monday, January 19th is Martin Luther King day in the US, so if you have that day off, it could make booking flights more convenient!

Hotels

click to show/hide ▼▲



There is a huge variety when it comes to accommodation in Cartagena, from the luxurious Hotel Santa Clara to the basic bed and breakfast Casa Carmen, to hostel El Viajero, the 2013 winner of Best Hostel in Latin America. The most beautiful and walkable region of the city is the Ciudad Amurallada (walled city), which is filled with cobblestone streets, gorgeous churches, restaurants, and other sights. Another option is the more modern Bocagrande neighborhood, a peninsula just south of the old walled city, which has the beach access that the walled city lacks. Bocagrande has more high rise hotels such as the Hampton, which has a rooftop pool! It's a short (less than 10 minutes) and cheap (less than 5 USD) taxi ride to the historic center from Bocagrande. TripAdvisor is a good site to browse reviews and photos of hotels throughout Cartagena

Cartagena also has many options if you’re looking to rent a house or apartment for your stay, from small 1 bedroom places to large houses for a large group. Airbnb has a great selection. Other sites to check are HomeAway, FlipKey, and COLuxury.

About Cartagena

click to show/hide ▼▲



Cartagena, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is the Venice of Colombia, with one of the most impressive old towns in the Western Hemisphere. With just the right mix of sun, sand, and colonial charm, a walk through Cartagena's inner walled city feels a bit like stepping onto the set of a 16th-century telenovela, complete with cobblestone streets and grandiose balconies overflowing with flowers.

Cartagena's tourism infrastructure is more developed than anywhere else in the country, so unlike some Colombian destinations, it's a pretty easy place to travel. People travel to Cartagena to splurge on its many fine hotels and restaurants, to explore its 500 years of history, and to sunbathe on its popular island beaches. You're sure to discover an enchanting place impossible to forget.

In January, Cartagena's weather is generally 75 degrees low - 85 degrees high, humid but quite breezy, with almost NO rain!

History

Founded by the Spanish in 1533, Cartagena is Colombia's and possibly Latin America's finest colonial city. Shortly after it was founded it became the main Spanish port on the Caribbean coast, and was used to store gold and other treasure plundered from the Indians before it was transported back to Spain.

Due to the riches stored within its walls, Cartagena quickly became a target, and was on the receiving end of countless pirate attacks and five full scale sieges in the 16th century alone. The most famous siege (although not the largest) was led by Sir Francis Drake in 1586.

After a while, fed up with all the attacks, the Spanish decided enough was enough, and made Cartagena virtually impregnable by building huge 12 km-long walls around the centre, and a series of forts and castles at strategic positions around the city.

Cartagena old town is a living museum of 16th and 17th century Spanish architecture, and it would easily be possible to spend a week or two here exploring the streets, admiring the beautiful colonial buildings, and soaking in the street life created by buskers, acrobats, dancers and artisans selling their wares. Not to mention the pristine Caribbean beaches, islands, and national parks within a day's reach of Cartagena.

The Ciudad Murallada

In 1987, Cartagena's Ciudad Murallada, or "Walled City", was declared a World Heritage Site by UNESCO, and its almost perfectly preserved colonial-era mansions, churches, and ornate balconies are the reason why. Where else in the Western Hemisphere can you sit in 16th-century plazas, walk along the walls of a 300-year-old fortress - one of the most impressive architectural feats of military history - and stay at a colonial-era hotel? Check TripAdvisor, but be sure not to miss the Plaza Santo Domingo, Plaza Bolivar, Plaza de la Aduana, and Torre del Reloj, as well as all the beautiful churches.

Colombia

click to show/hide ▼▲



Why not extend your trip to explore more of Colombia? It's an extremely diverse country, with beaches on both the Atlantic and Pacific coasts, the Andes mountains, jungles, plains, deserts, coffee plantations...and more! Even spending an extra day or so in Bogotá on your way to or from Cartagena is well worth the effort.

From Cartagena, you can fly directly to Bogota, to Cali for some salsa, to beautiful Medellin, or San Andres Island for some snorkeling!

Citizens of the United States do not need a visa to visit Colombia, just a passport! Colombia uses the same power system and plugs as the US. No adapters needed. As long as you alert your bank beforehand, you can easily withdraw cash using your ATM card, and this way you'll get the best exchange rate as well. If you have T-Mobile, you get free unlimited texting and 3G data in Colombia, but if not, we recommend installing WhatsApp or Google Hangouts on your phone to communicate using wifi.
